About Me

I was the operator. Now I'm out front.

I'm Diane Corriette, and for most of my career I was the operator — the person behind the scenes who ran everything. I built other people's websites, systems and online presence while they stood at the front. I was good at it, and it paid well.

I don't talk about money much, but I'll be plain about it, because people keep mistaking me for a beginner: I've made good money, over and over. I'm not waiting to make my first £5k. I've done that several times. The catch was always the same, though - every penny came from selling my time. The day I stopped, the money stopped.

So this is what I'm building now: monthly recurring income from a single site selling digital products. No stage, no teaching, no group coaching - nothing that trades my hours for income, unless I choose to. A handful of sites that sell on their own, and a small group of people who genuinely love what I do. My 1,000 true fans.

To build it, I had to come out from behind the screen. At 57 I learned to code and built Djangify, the system my sites run on, with AI as my assistant. Now I'm the one out front — building in public and documenting the climb, wins and mistakes both left in. Not a five-year plan. Just the next thing, in service of owning something that's finally mine.

What I Do

Three things. All of them real.

Developer & Builder

I build with Python, Django, Flask, and TailwindCSS. My focus is clean, self-hosted, offline-friendly tools — software people can actually own, not rent. I build and maintain projects under the Djangify organisation on GitHub.

Founder of Djangify

Djangify is an eCommerce system for professionals who sell knowledge, expertise and digital products. Available as managed hosting at £12/month or a one-time self-hosted purchase. Built so creators own their store, their data, and their profits — with no platform taking a cut of every sale.

Personal Growth Coach

27 years coaching — NLP Master Practitioner and EFT. I coach through Inspirational Guidance, which I built from nothing and now run on the system I built myself — proof the "just the next thing" approach works. My superpower is making people feel seen, and I don't manufacture it. It happens when I show up as myself.

The Human Bit

The things that don't fit neatly on a CV.

My parents were from Dominica — a small island in the Caribbean — and that heritage shaped how I think about community, about showing up for people, and about building things that actually belong to the people who use them. The social network I built in 2009 was for the Dominican diaspora. When you want to connect people who are scattered across the world and far from home, you build something real or you don't bother.

I'm an INFJ. The profile says: "Fuelled by a profound internal passion. Stimulated by deep, reflective thought and an enormous amount of empathy. Conscientious to the core, they move through life with a clear sense of their values." That's accurate. It's also why I coach as well as build — the two things aren't separate for me.

I write. I have flash fiction and poetry going back years, self-published ebooks on self-confidence, paperback notebooks on Amazon, and GCSE study books. I also sell non-fiction. Writing and building are both just different ways of making something from nothing, and I've never really been able to stop doing either.
