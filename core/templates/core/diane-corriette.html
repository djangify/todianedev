About Me

I build the tools I wish existed.

I'm Diane Corriette — a full-stack Django developer, founder of Djangify, life coach, and digital creator. I've been building things online since 2005, and I have never once considered stopping.

I didn't take the conventional route into tech. I came through years of building websites for clients, running membership communities, studying digital marketing, and eventually making a deliberate decision in 2023 to retrain as a software developer. I graduated with a Merit in Full Stack Software Development (eCommerce) from Code Institute in March 2024.

Djangify came directly from that journey — a platform I built because the tools I needed didn't exist in the form I wanted them, and because I was tired of watching independent creators hand their profits and their customer data to platforms that don't know their name.

What I Do

Three things. All of them real.

Developer & Builder

I build with Python, Django, Flask, and TailwindCSS. My focus is clean, self-hosted, offline-friendly tools — software people can actually own, not rent. I build and maintain projects under the Djangify organisation on GitHub.

Founder of Djangify

Djangify is a Django-based eCommerce platform for independent creators selling digital products. Available as managed hosting at £12/month or a one-time self-hosted purchase. Built so creators own their store, their data, and their profits — with no platform taking a cut of every sale.

Life Coach

I coach through my brand Inspirational Guidance — a brand that has been active for years and is not for sale. My superpower is making people feel seen, and I don't manufacture it. It happens when I show up as myself.

The Human Bit

The things that don't fit neatly on a CV.

My parents were from Dominica — a small island in the Caribbean — and that heritage shaped how I think about community, about showing up for people, and about building things that actually belong to the people who use them. The social network I built in 2009 was for the Dominican diaspora. When you want to connect people who are scattered across the world and far from home, you build something real or you don't bother.

I'm an INFJ. The profile says: "Fuelled by a profound internal passion. Stimulated by deep, reflective thought and an enormous amount of empathy. Conscientious to the core, they move through life with a clear sense of their values." That's accurate. It's also why I coach as well as build — the two things aren't separate for me.

I write. I have flash fiction and poetry going back years, self-published ebooks on self-confidence, paperback notebooks on Amazon, and GCSE study books. I also sell non-fiction. Writing and building are both just different ways of making something from nothing, and I've never really been able to stop doing either.

"My superpower is making people feel seen, and I don't manufacture it — it just happens when I show up as myself."

I've been in rooms full of people performing success while quietly wondering if I was the only one who didn't have it figured out. I'm not talking at people when I say that. I'm talking to them. That's where everything I build comes from — not a strategy, just who I am.
