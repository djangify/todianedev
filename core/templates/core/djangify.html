Why Not Just Use Shopify or Gumroad?

You could. Many creators do. But those platforms take a cut of every sale, set the rules you operate under, hold your customer data in their database, and can change their pricing or terms at any time. If they raise fees or shut down a feature you depend on, you feel it immediately — because you're a tenant on their platform, not an owner.

Djangify is fundamentally different. Your store runs as its own independent instance. Your customer data lives in your database. Payments go directly from your customer to your Stripe account. No platform sitting in the middle taking its share.

Djangify sits in the middle — more professional than a PayPal link, far more ownership-friendly than SaaS marketplaces, and entirely yours to run however suits your business.

Why This Isn't SaaS — And Why That Matters

SaaS means the software runs on the vendor's servers, everyone is on the same shared platform, and your access depends on your subscription continuing. Stop paying and your store disappears. The vendor can raise prices, change their terms, or shut down features at any time.

Djangify is different at a fundamental level. Whether you're on managed hosting or self-hosted, you have your own dedicated instance — not a seat on a shared platform. Your customer data lives in your database. Your store runs independently. The software doesn't depend on Djangify's continued existence to keep running.

The people Djangify is built for have often already felt the frustration of SaaS dependency — platforms that raise transaction fees, change payout rules, restrict customisation, or shut down with little notice. Djangify offers a different relationship with the software running your business. You're not a tenant. You're an owner.
